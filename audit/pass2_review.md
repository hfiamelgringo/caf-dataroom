# Stakeholder Interview Library — Editorial Review for CAF

**Executive summary.** The 107 findings span **52 distinct targets** across governments, funds, DFIs, accelerators, and ecosystem actors. This review recommends **0 CUT**, **14 SOFTEN**, and **38 KEEP** across the sections below (the remainder are REVIEW). No finding rises to the level of pure gossip or ad hominem; most criticisms are grounded in observable institutional behaviour or market structure. Several patterns warrant flagging before submission to CAF: **(1) CAF itself is named in five findings** — two moderate-severity process critiques from named stakeholders and one integrity-adjacent recommendation to hire external management; these are essential for self-assessment but should be framed as constructive. **(2) DFIs as a category draw five independent moderate-to-sharp criticisms** from three separate stakeholders (Aboyeji, Meneses, Menendez, and the Central American roundtable), making the "too slow, process-driven, market-distorting" thesis load-bearing and analytically defensible. **(3) Parque Tec is named across three sources** with a concrete harm narrative (the iNova cap-table damage); this cluster is significant. **(4) Honduras and the Dominican Republic each receive convergent multi-source critiques of political capture** that should be retained as ecosystem-context findings. **(5)** The "Mexican fund managers optimising for fees" critique (Menendez) and the Corfo rent-seeking finding (Zavala) each use sharp language that should be softened before sharing with a bank audience, without losing the substance.
