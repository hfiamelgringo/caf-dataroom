# Introduction and Background

This report constitutes Deliverable 1 of the Partnership for the Americas (PFA) engagement with the Development Bank of Latin America and the Caribbean (CAF). It presents a regulatory and ecosystem roadmap for venture capital development in Guatemala, Costa Rica, the Dominican Republic and Honduras. The purpose of the document is to identify specific regulatory gaps and propose actionable reforms to unlock capital flows and strengthen legal certainty for local and international investors in these markets. This deliverable represents a working analysis that will continue to be refined in subsequent phases of the engagement. Some hypotheses and preliminary recommendations presented here will be tested, revised, and deepened in the subsequent deliverables based on additional consultations and feedback from CAF.

Overall, the diagnostic elements in this report are not likely to surprise, and the prescriptive elements include no obvious "quick fixes," given the complexity in impacting the entrepreneurial ecosystems and the economic realities underpinning the weak investment data in smaller economies.

To address CAF's interest in concrete and focused recommendations that leverage its comparative advantages, this report does not attempt to present an exhaustive list of ecosystem needs over the next generation, an ambition that would exceed what CAF's resources alone can reasonably achieve. Instead, PFA focused on recommendations that allow CAF to demonstrate a regional leadership role in a scaled and systematic manner that require modest financial and staff commitments. In that spirit, PFA provides a short list of options for CAF to explore one or two of them as areas of focus in the next 12 months that position CAF to claim a strategic, relevant, and central role in ecosystem building in the thinnest markets across the region.

PFA does not discuss the CAF's FIDE fund or the recently announced FIIALC fund in this document, but will seek to make connections to those efforts in subsequent deliverables.
