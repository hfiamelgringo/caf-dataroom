## Honduras

Honduras is the smallest economy among the four countries by GDP (USD 37 billion) and has experienced the most limited venture ecosystem development. The economy is heavily dependent on agriculture, remittances, and tourism. Institutional and regulatory capacity is the weakest among the four countries, and capital market infrastructure is minimal.

### Macroeconomic Context

Honduras's economy has grown at an average annual rate of 2.0 percent over the past decade, the lowest among the four countries. The economy is heavily dependent on agriculture (bananas, coffee), remittances, and tourism. Manufacturing capacity is limited, and the financial system is underdeveloped. FDI has been volatile but has averaged 2-3 percent of GDP annually. ([World Bank, 2024d](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/NY.GDP.MKTP.CD?locations=HN))

### Venture Capital Market

Venture capital investment in Honduras is minimal and sporadic. Available data indicate that annual VC investment has been less than USD 10 million in most years, concentrated in a handful of companies and foreign investors. Domestic venture investment is virtually nonexistent, and the ecosystem lacks experienced venture investors and fund managers. ([Cuántico VP & Startuplinks, 2026](https://www.startuplinks.world/reportes/latin-america-venture-capital-report-2025/))

### Entrepreneurial Ecosystem

Tegucigalpa hosts most entrepreneurial activity, though the volume remains limited. A handful of accelerators and incubators operate in the country, but ecosystem infrastructure is minimal. Entrepreneurs report significant challenges in accessing capital, technical expertise, and networks. The ecosystem lacks critical density to support rapid growth.

*“Honduras faces the steepest climb. The economy is smaller, institutional capacity is weaker, and the ecosystem infrastructure is minimal. But that also means the opportunity to build something from the ground up is significant. CAF could play an outsized role here.” — Interview, Regional Expert, 2026*

### Regulatory Environment

Honduras's regulatory environment for venture capital is underdeveloped. Securities regulations are outdated and provide minimal guidance on venture capital structures. The Comisión Nacional de Bancos y Seguros (CNBS) lacks the institutional capacity to provide regulatory guidance on venture capital. Comprehensive regulatory reform is required, and CAF technical assistance could support Honduras in developing supportive regulatory frameworks. (*Decreto No. 8-2001; Decreto 83-2021*)

### Pension Fund Landscape

Honduras's pension fund system is mandatory private, with approximately 700,000 contributors and managed assets of approximately USD 4 billion. Pension fund allocation to alternative investments is minimal. However, given the small asset base, pension fund reform alone will be insufficient to address capital constraints.

### Capital Markets Infrastructure

Honduras's Bolsa de Valores is underdeveloped with limited liquidity. Capital market capitalization is less than 5 percent of GDP. Capital market infrastructure does not currently support venture ecosystem development. Regulatory and institutional capacity building would be required to develop secondary markets and exit infrastructure.
