## Regional Funds of Funds

One of the primary structural limitations in the four target countries is the absence of large, professionally managed local VC funds that can aggregate capital and deploy at scale. Most VC activity in the region is conducted by foreign investors or small local funds with limited capital under management. The absence of large local funds creates a structural mismatch between the scale of capital required and the capital available locally.

Regional funds of funds, organized and capitalized by multilateral development banks, have proven effective in emerging markets. CAF could consider establishing a regional fund of funds that:

* Provides co-investment capital to expand the capacity of high-performing local VC funds
* Establishes standardized investment criteria, due diligence processes, and governance frameworks across the region
* Attracts international limited partners (LPs) to deploy capital across the region at scale
* Builds the institutional capacity of local fund managers over time

  *“Anchor checks matter. When international institutional investors see that a development bank has committed significant capital to a regional fund of funds, it signals confidence and dramatically increases the likelihood of subsequent institutional investor participation.” — Interview, Senior VC Investor, 2026*
