## Dominican Republic

The Dominican Republic is the largest economy among the four countries by GDP (USD 110 billion) but has experienced the most limited venture ecosystem development. The country's economy is heavily dependent on tourism and remittances, which collectively account for over 50 percent of GDP. The financial system is underdeveloped relative to the size of the economy, and capital market infrastructure is minimal.

### Macroeconomic Context

The Dominican Republic's economy has grown at an average annual rate of 4.5 percent over the past decade, the highest among the four countries, driven primarily by tourism, construction, and remittance-based consumption. However, underlying economic fundamentals are weaker than growth rates suggest, with limited manufacturing capacity and heavy dependence on commodity prices and external capital flows. FDI has been relatively stable at 2-3 percent of GDP annually. ([World Bank, 2024c](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/NY.GDP.MKTP.CD?locations=DO))

### Venture Capital Market

Venture capital investment in the Dominican Republic has been minimal and episodic. Available data indicate that annual VC investment has ranged from USD 10-30 million over the past three years, concentrated in a small number of fintech and software companies. Most investment has been sourced from foreign investors with no meaningful domestic institutional investment in venture capital. ([Cuántico VP & Startuplinks, 2026](https://www.startuplinks.world/reportes/latin-america-venture-capital-report-2025/))

### Entrepreneurial Ecosystem

Santo Domingo dominates entrepreneurial activity and VC investment. The city has a nascent ecosystem with a handful of accelerators and incubators, but ecosystem development is less advanced than in Costa Rica or Guatemala. Entrepreneurs report challenges in accessing capital and limited availability of mentorship and technical support tailored to their needs.

*“The Dominican Republic has entrepreneurial potential but limited ecosystem infrastructure. Accelerators are newer and less mature. Capital is scarce. The ecosystem lacks the density of experienced founders and investors that would support rapid ecosystem growth.” — Interview, Dominican Ecosystem Expert, 2026*

### Regulatory Environment

The Dominican Republic's regulatory environment is the least developed among the four countries. Securities regulations are outdated and were not designed for modern venture capital structures. The Superintendencia de Valores (SIV) lacks clear guidance on venture capital fund formation and investment structures. Tax law creates significant uncertainties for venture investors and entrepreneurs. Comprehensive regulatory reform is required to create a supportive environment for venture ecosystem development. (*Ley No. 249-17; Ley No. 87-01*)

### Pension Fund Landscape

The Dominican Republic's pension fund system is mandatory and private, with approximately 2.8 million contributors and managed assets of approximately USD 30 billion. Pension fund allocation to alternative investments is minimal (less than 0.05 percent of assets). However, recent regulatory discussions have explored the possibility of pension fund participation in venture capital and private equity. The large asset base represents significant potential for ecosystem development if allocation policies are reformed.

### Capital Markets Infrastructure

The Dominican Republic's Bolsa de Valores is underdeveloped, with limited liquidity and minimal secondary market activity. Market capitalization is less than 10 percent of GDP. Capital market infrastructure does not currently support venture ecosystem development, and regulatory reform would be required to develop secondary markets for startup equity. ([World Bank, 2024e](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/CM.MKT.LCAP.GD.ZS))
