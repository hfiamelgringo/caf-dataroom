# Overall Insights and Framing

## Introduction and Framing

Historically, ecosystem-building efforts in the region have focused on supply-side interventions: training programs for entrepreneurs, accelerators and incubators, networking events, and direct grants or investments in early-stage companies. While these programs have generated meaningful activity and some notable successes, the broader ecosystem metrics have remained stagnant. The persistence of thin market conditions despite years of ecosystem-building efforts suggests that supply-side interventions alone are insufficient.

A more effective framing shifts toward market formation and structural incentives. Rather than training entrepreneurs or funding their companies directly, the focus should be on constructing the market conditions under which entrepreneurship becomes a rational and scalable outcome. This requires attention to the institutional, regulatory, and incentive structures that govern capital flows, exits, talent mobility, and information asymmetries.

## Methodology

This assessment evaluates the venture capital ecosystem in the four countries through two primary lenses: (1) the regulatory environment governing venture capital, private equity, and startup investment; and (2) the broader entrepreneurial ecosystem, including market structure, available capital, and investor incentives. \[PLACEHOLDER: Annex reference\] provides the detailed regulatory inventory by country and includes a comprehensive review of securities regulations, tax policy, corporate law, and sectoral regulations that govern VC activity.

The analysis methodology combined desk research, stakeholder interviews, and quantitative analysis of investment data. The assessment team conducted over 80 interviews with entrepreneurs, investors, government officials, and ecosystem participants in the four countries. The analysis also leveraged existing data from LAVCA, Cuántico VP, Startuplinks, and other regional data providers to quantify ecosystem metrics including capital flows, exit activity, and unicorn creation.
