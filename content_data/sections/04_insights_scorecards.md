## Comparative Analysis

### Ecosystem Scorecard

| Country | Capital Access | Regulatory | Human Capital | Infrastructure | Exits | Ecosystem Support |
| ----- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Guatemala | 2/5 | 2/5 | 2/5 | 2/5 | 1/5 | 3/5 |
| Costa Rica | 3/5 | 3/5 | 3/5 | 3/5 | 2/5 | 4/5 |
| Dominican Republic | 2/5 | 2/5 | 2/5 | 2/5 | 1/5 | 2/5 |
| Honduras | 1/5 | 2/5 | 2/5 | 2/5 | 1/5 | 2/5 |

Costa Rica leads across most ecosystem dimensions, particularly in regulatory clarity and ecosystem support infrastructure. The country has benefited from sustained ecosystem-building efforts over the past decade and has a relatively mature accelerator and incubator ecosystem. Costa Rica also has the most developed capital market infrastructure, with the Bolsa Nacional de Valores serving as a potential venue for secondary market development.

The regulatory gap matrix illustrates that while major regulatory gaps exist across the four countries, they are most pronounced in Honduras and Dominican Republic. Guatemala presents a mixed profile with moderate gaps in some areas. Costa Rica demonstrates the most developed regulatory framework, though even there, opportunities exist for targeted regulatory reform to support venture ecosystem development.
