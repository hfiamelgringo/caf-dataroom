## Bridge to Silicon Valley

CAF could consider developing a modest, systematic program to facilitate connections between early-stage entrepreneurs in the four countries and Silicon Valley investors and accelerators. The program would:

* Organize annual or semi-annual delegations of entrepreneurs and investors to Silicon Valley to participate in major VC events and pitch competitions
* Provide seed-stage funding for entrepreneurs selected for participation in top-tier accelerators like Y Combinator, Techstars, or 500 Global
* Facilitate introductions between regional entrepreneurs and Silicon Valley investors, venture capital firms, and strategic partners
* **Build a curated Silicon Valley ecosystem map** covering venture capital firms, corporate venture arms, accelerators, and university innovation labs with active or latent interest in the four priority markets — giving regional entrepreneurs and fund managers a practical, continuously updated entry point rather than a cold-start approach
* **Host recurring closed-door investor roundtables** that bring together Silicon Valley LPs and GPs with LAC-focused counterparts, anchored institutionally through partnerships with Stanford or UC Berkeley to provide credibility, infrastructure, and access to frontier research networks
* **Mobilize the Latin American tech diaspora** in the Bay Area — founders, operators, and investors with roots in the region — as a structured connective layer for deal flow, mentorship, and co-investment, complemented by quarterly intelligence briefs on Silicon Valley trends relevant to the four markets

While these efforts may seem modest in comparison to larger structural reforms, they can have outsized impact on ecosystem perception and participation. Access to Silicon Valley networks and capital is a critical success factor for entrepreneurs in emerging markets. CAF's convening power and relationships could facilitate these connections at modest cost.
