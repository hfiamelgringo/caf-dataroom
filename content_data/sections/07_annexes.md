## Annex B: Capital Market Shallowness

Capital market development is a key prerequisite for venture ecosystem maturation. Shallow capital markets constrain exit opportunities, limit investor returns, and reduce incentives for venture investment. The following analysis details capital market infrastructure in the four countries and identifies opportunities for targeted development.

![Title: 04\_capital\_market\_depth.png - Description: 04\_capital\_market\_depth.png][image7]

The analysis reveals significant disparities in capital market development across the four countries. Costa Rica's market capitalization as a percentage of GDP (approximately 18%) is substantially higher than Honduras (approximately 5%). However, even Costa Rica's capital market remains shallow compared to developed markets and more mature emerging markets. Liquidity, breadth of listed companies, and depth of trading activity are limited across all four countries.

# Orphan Insights

## Dominican Republic Regulatory Reform Opportunities

The Dominican Republic stands to benefit most significantly from targeted regulatory reform. The country's securities regulations are the least developed among the four countries, and modernization would create a more supportive environment for venture capital investment. Key reform areas include: (1) modernization of securities regulation to explicitly address venture capital structures; (2) clarification of tax treatment for venture capital investments and carried interest; (3) development of secondary market frameworks and mechanisms; and (4) regulatory guidance on alternative investment participation by institutional investors including pension funds and insurance companies. (*Ley No. 249-17; Ley No. 87-01*)

## Dedicated Venture Capital Fund Regulation

All four countries would benefit from the establishment of a dedicated regulatory framework for venture capital funds. Rather than subjecting venture capital funds to the same regulatory requirements as traditional securities funds or private equity funds, a dedicated VC framework could reduce regulatory burden while maintaining investor protections. Such a framework might include simplified licensing requirements for small funds, streamlined reporting and disclosure requirements, and clear regulatory guidance on permissible investments and permissible investor types. ([White & Case, 2025](https://www.whitecase.com/insight-our-thinking/global-ipo-market-2025-latam))
