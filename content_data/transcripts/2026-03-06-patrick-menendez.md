- Patrick Menendez, Investment Director at FEMSA Ventures
- 10 years DC-LATAM experience, previously Varys Capital/Cometa

- Platform team of 2 full-time connecting startups with FEMSA business units across geographies
- Internal alignment issues
- CFO changes create funding vulnerability - previous CFO championed program, current one doesn’t
- Business unit operators question why VC gets $100M when they struggle with resources
- Misaligned incentives between operators (short-term) and VC timeline (10+ years)
- Structural problems
- Difficult to maintain programs when parent company performance declines
- Internal politics around decision-making authority between business units and VC team
- Takes months/years for large organizations to warm up to startup collaboration programs
- Latin America VC Ecosystem Analysis
- Mexico market dysfunction
- No deal flow from Mexican founders - mostly Chilean, Colombian, Uruguayan startups
- Major funds unable to close after 2+ years of fundraising
- Impossible to responsibly deploy $100M+ funds in Spanish-speaking LATAM
- No path to return $500M-3B across multiple large funds
- One unicorn every 10-15 years (MercadoLibre, Nubank, maybe Despegar)
- Fund size problems
- Mexican funds jumping from $20M to $100M+ without gradual scaling
- Brazilian funds building sustainable firms vs. Mexican “boutique shops” extracting value
- FEMSA Ventures refused to invest in Mexican funds over $80M hard cap
- Multilateral Development Bank Issues
- IDB investment experience
- Months/years to get commitments - significant administrative drag
- Tendency to keep funding same managers rather than seeking new talent
- Some championed managers underperforming despite continued support
- Recommendations for improvement
- Streamline processes for faster deployment
- Graduate funds after 3-4 iterations to make room for new managers
- Focus on responsible fund sizing rather than large vehicles
