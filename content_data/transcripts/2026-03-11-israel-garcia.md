## Context
Israel Garcia is the founder of Startuplinks, a platform aggregating data and connecting startups, investors, and accelerators across Spanish-speaking Latin America, with ambitions to become the PitchBook/Crunchbase of the region. This interview covers structural challenges in the LATAM VC ecosystem — especially liquidity and exit gaps affecting small countries — as well as fintech and AI trends, and Startuplinks' own growth via events like Mexico Tech Week and a proprietary VC database, making it a useful regional benchmark for the CAF project's focus countries.

## Notes
- Platform connecting LATAM startup ecosystem (excluding Brazil due to Spanish focus)
- Three core functions:
- Founder support through events, tools, resources, video content, podcasts (mostly free)
- Connecting startups with investors, accelerators, corporates across Spanish-speaking LATAM
- Data aggregation and reporting on ecosystem metrics
- Long-term goal: become “PitchBook/Crunchbase for LATAM”
- Building SaaS product currently
- Eventually launch own VC firm leveraging platform data
- LATAM VC Ecosystem Challenges
- Small countries face education gap on venture capital asset class
- Need people who’ve lived the experience (sold companies, run accelerators)
- Alternative is expensive travel to Silicon Valley, Israel, NYC
- Major liquidity problem across region
- Exits taking 15-20 years on average vs 10 years in mature markets
- Without founder/investor liquidity, VC model breaks down
- Examples: Despegar (Argentina), but limited exit activity overall
- Investor pessimism vs founder optimism
- VCs concerned about exits and liquidity
- Founders remain optimistic, willing to bootstrap without VC funding
- Fintech dominance continues in 2026
- Credit cards for companies/individuals still leading (Clara, Clip, Jeeves)
- Cross-border payments emerging (crypto-based solutions)
- Peru’s Yape became verb for payments (“voy a yapearte”)
- AI adoption focused on implementation, not creation
- Using OpenAI, Gemini, Anthropic rather than building from scratch
- Success stories in fine-tuning existing models
- Call center/voice agent applications gaining corporate traction
- Agent boom creating confusion due to too many options
- Mexico Tech Week largest in LATAM (4 years running)
- Attracts participants from across region, US, Brazil
- 2026 plans: Founder House, VC House, third house TBD, possibly AI House
- Colombia Tech Week partners with local GoFest event
- Venezuela Tech Week launching May 2026 (centralized format)
- Mexico Fintech Week recently concluded (first dedicated fintech week)
- Model varies by country based on existing ecosystem maturity
- WhatsApp channel: ~300 VCs
- Reverse pitch events: 150+ VCs, accelerators, angel groups participated
- Published Mexican VC study with Quantico
- Building comprehensive database for live ecosystem mapping
- Next week: scheduled call with 4 Central American VCs
