### Context

7–8 years in VC at CDC Banco de la Vivienda (Colombia) and Wayra/Telefónica (regional, ex-Brazil), with hands-on M&A, buybacks, and partial-exit experience. **Petra** is a 6-week-old LatAm secondary brokerage. Co-founders: a Dentons partner and a New York investment banker.

### Key Findings

- **8 deals in pipeline, 0 closed yet**; ~200 inbound seller offers in the first 6 weeks — supply far exceeds active matched demand
- Working transaction sizes: **$1–5M secondary positions**
- Cannot serve large positions. Cometa wants to sell $30M of Bitso — but the entire largest Colombian fund is $30M total. There is no domestic buyer pool at this scale
- Capital-flow data: **~70% of capital entering LatAm is foreign; only ~30% is local**. LatAm fund fundraising barely reaches $1B/year vs. ~$4B inbound — "the buyer money is abroad"
- Strategic next step: launching a **$500M closed-end public-listed vehicle** holding ~10 LatAm portfolio companies — modeled on Bill Ackman's Pershing Square emerging-markets approach. Target: scale to $5–10B AUM in a few years
- Fundraising to build the vehicle: **$3M for ~12 months** of regulatory, legal, banking, market-maker, and analyst infrastructure

### Regulatory Observations

- The LatAm secondary market is structurally **buyer-supply mismatched** — rich seller pipeline, anemic local buyer base. This is a regulatory + capital-formation gap, not a deal-quality gap
- Vehicle structuring requires US/UK securities-law expertise (hence Dentons + NY banking co-founders)

### Recommendations & Insights

- **Secondaries are now THE liquidity vehicle for LatAm VC** — primary M&A and IPOs are too rare to satisfy DPI demand from Fund 1 / Fund 2 LPs
- The **emerging-markets globalization wave is finally reaching LatAm VC** as an asset class — "globalization started 50 years ago; emerging markets' turn has arrived"
- Long-term ambition: democratize emerging-markets investing across VC → credit → infrastructure → energy
