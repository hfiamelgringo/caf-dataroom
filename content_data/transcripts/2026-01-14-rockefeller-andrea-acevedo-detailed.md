## Context
Strategic alignment call between PFA leadership (Mark Lopes, Luis Arreaga, Geoffrey Kirkman, Rigoberto Romero, Natasha Ibarra) and Rockefeller Foundation's regional office (Andrea Acevedo + Lyana Latorre). Rockefeller's regional office (Bogotá, ~1 year operating) is mapping potential partnerships in Central America, where Rockefeller has not had deep prior intervention. The conversation surfaces Rockefeller's three thematic lines, its "scale existing initiatives" funding philosophy, and concludes with a concrete agreement to design a Central America climate-migration convening — the core deliverable from this meeting.

## Summary

Andrea Acevedo described Rockefeller's regional approach focused on **energy, health, and food systems**, with a global "big bet" on **School Meals** ($100M committed, 100M children target) and a regional differentiator on **climate migration** in Central America. Funding strategy is to scale existing initiatives via subregional partners with private-sector co-participation — not open calls, not greenfield projects. Both sides identified strong potential for Rockefeller programs in Central America and agreed to organize a **convening on climate migration** (with a regenerative-agriculture component) — likely in Guatemala in Q2 2026 — as the first concrete step. Follow-up scheduled for Feb 11.

## Detailed Notes

### Rockefeller's regional structure
- Bogotá office, ~1 year operating; intentionally NOT pan-regional due to budget. Sub-divided into México, Brasil, Colombia, Caribe, Centroamérica, Suramérica.
- Phase 1 focus: Colombia and Brazil. Phase 2 expansion target: Central America (where Rockefeller has historically only done limited grants).

### Three thematic lines
1. **Energy** — focused on "energy abundance" / first-access scenarios in off-grid communities. Reference model: "Mission 300" in Africa. Goal is energy as an enabler for education, health, connectivity — not just generation.
2. **Health** — climate-driven health impacts (heat waves, floods). Active project in Central America: heat-and-health pilot at sugarcane mills, working with private sector (the mill itself) to mitigate heat impact on workers — initially in El Salvador, replicable across the region.
3. **Food Systems** — the "big bet": **School Meals**, $100M global commitment to reach 100M children. Rockefeller does NOT pay for school meals directly (that's a government obligation); funds the surrounding ecosystem — small farmers supplying schools, regenerative agriculture, school gardens. Existing Honduras grant via FAO / World Food Program.

### Funding philosophy — "no open calls"
- No open calls. Rockefeller actively seeks subregional partners with deep local experience.
- Goal: scale / amplify existing initiatives that need a push, not start projects from zero.
- Reference report (DBier-produced) on international cooperation funding in the region surfaced 5 agendas guiding the regional office's work.
- Innovative Finance team is internally restructuring; deferred for now but will be a regional capability later.

### Central America potential — three differentiators identified
- **Health (sugarcane mill heat-and-health)** — already piloting; replication potential across Central America.
- **Energy access** — many remote-community needs, partial overlap with rural development.
- **Climate migration** — flagged as the **most differentiated angle** for the LATAM region (not what Africa or Asia teams do). Acevedo's clear preference for a Central America focus on climate migration. Partial overlap with the existing health project. Luis Arreaga confirmed Guatemala has documented climate-driven migration tied to the "corredor seco" (dry corridor) drought patterns.

### Existing Honduras footprint — PFA's strategic value
Rigoberto Romero detailed PFA's existing Honduras relationships:
- **FAO + Secretaría de Agricultura y Ganadería**: FAO presented PFA with 4 prepared investment notes (honey, coffee, bean seed, others) — fully developed projects with budgets and stages. PFA has worked with FAO on agricultural sector briefings, mapping key stakeholders.
- **COEP (Consejo Hondureño de la Empresa Privada)** — umbrella body for private-sector federations in Honduras. PFA has run joint events with COEP's enterprise-development arm, which supports projects in agriculture, AI, new tech.
- Active partner companies: **Agroamérica, Mariposa** (regenerative agriculture, agribusiness).
- **World Food Program**: working with Salvadoran government on school feeding for isolated schools — likely overlap with CAF funding.

### Private-sector partnership model
- Acevedo emphasized that in Latin America, Rockefeller partners with the **private sector as a partner (not as charity)** — distinct from Rockefeller's US/Africa model where they work mostly with governments.
- Goal: "win-win for private sector + win-win for community". Important amid government turnover risk: 2026–2027 will see administration changes across the region.

### Convening proposal — agreed deliverable
- **Format**: a "convening" (not a giant open conference), focused, semi-formal, with curated stakeholders.
- **Topic**: climate migration, with a regenerative agriculture / agricultural sustainability component.
- **Location**: likely Guatemala (Luis Arreaga: "fertile ground despite political polarization; PFA has previously sat private sector + civil society + government in respectful dialogue"; previously almost achieved this with resilient agriculture but government changes derailed it).
- **Timing**: Q2 2026 — Acevedo prefers May; not Q1 (Mark Lopes proposed March, Acevedo deferred).
- **Outcomes targeted** (based on Brazil/Colombia/Mexico precedent):
  1. **Mapping** — published artifact for the ecosystem on who's working on climate migration in Central America.
  2. **Cross-org commitment** — 3–4 organizations committing to a joint follow-up project after the convening (Rockefeller would participate, not lead).
- Rockefeller will NOT fund a megaproject from this; budget is modest ("low hundreds of thousands"). Mark Lopes flagged willingness to think bigger if there's appetite.
- PFA companies-of-interest cited as potential participants: **Fundación Terra** (works closely with PFA, school-focused), Agroamérica, Mariposa.
