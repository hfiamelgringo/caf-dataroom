- Guatemala Venture Ecosystem Assessment
- Regulatory barriers as primary weakness
- No fintech sandbox - complex, expensive bank establishment process functions like cartel
- Lacks clear innovation-incentivizing regulation
- 90% of entrepreneurship is necessity-based, not high-impact innovation
- Government coordination issues
- Multiple uncoordinated entities: innovation secretariat, economy ministry, competitiveness program
- No integrated approach like Colombia or Chile’s Corfo program
- Cultural and educational gaps
- Conservative society - professionals still aspire to corporate jobs
- Universities have traditional entrepreneurship centers with professors who never founded startups
- No university serves as innovation hub for venture/high-impact entrepreneurship
- Capital Availability vs Talent Gap
- Capital exists but lacks suitable investment targets
- Castillo Hermanos acquired Sony Delight for $1.5B (US)
- Corporación Multiinversiones invested $75M in Israeli 3D meat printing startup
- Local family offices investing in foreign startups entering Guatemala market
- Foreign startups successfully raising local capital
- Airbag (Impacta portfolio) securing major Guatemalan client/investor
- La Guagua (Venezuelan founders) and Boxful (Salvadoran) both locally funded
- Chicken-and-egg problem: entrepreneurs leave, investors lack local expertise
- Recommended Solutions
- Attract global talent through incentive programs
- Follow Start-up Chile model: $100K + growth capital for relocating founders
- El Salvador’s Cornest partnership: government matching fund contributions
- Honduras’ Prospera: $40M round for health startup in Roatán with tax incentives
- Focus on fintech sector specifically
- 60% of LATAM venture investment ($3B) went to fintech last year
- Guatemala’s 18M population ideal for experimentation (similar to Chile)
- Regulatory sandbox needed for financial innovation
- Institutional leadership model
- Kazakhstan example: major bank sponsored $1B innovation hub
- Brazil model: pension funds + FOMIN created $2B patient capital vehicle
- Guatemala Business Environment
- Incorporation process

- Services to US companies from Guatemala exempt from both countries’ taxes
- Political challenges

- Traditional commodity sectors (sugar, coffee) lobby for their own incentives
