## Context
José Kont, Director at Cuántico VP, brings 20+ years of experience spanning startup founding, ORM, media, and VC-focused market research in Latin America. This interview draws on findings from a Peru VC ecosystem study and José's regional perspective to diagnose why capital exists but scalable startups don't in Guatemala and Central America — and what public institutions like CAF can do to close the gap.

## Notes
- Introductions and Background
- José Kont, Director at Cuántico VP
- 20+ years experience, entered tech industry 2005
- Co-founded startup in 2012, first Central American company in Wayra Mexico (2nd cohort)
- Founded leading ORM agency in 2019, serves major Latin American conglomerates
- Cuántico VP operates at convergence of three areas:
- Market research - addressing Latin America’s data scarcity
- Media communications - 8 owned media properties, partnerships with 3 others
- Venture capital industry focus - promoting economic development through VC
- Peru VC Ecosystem Study Findings
- Study conducted with TKAP (Peru VC Association), Swiss cooperation, and Cofide development bank
- Goal: understand how to accelerate/double Peru’s VC investment
- Key barriers identified:
- Information gap for non-local investors
- Language barriers
- Limited cross-border knowledge (even neighboring countries)
- Historical local media monopoly structure
- Political instability concerns
- Investors prioritize stability, legal certainty, strong institutions over tax incentives
- El Salvador security improvements attracted new funds
- Largest family offices/patrimonies not participating in VC
- Agricultural/traditional business fortunes avoiding innovation investments
- Lack of education about asset class benefits
- Regulatory gaps
- Pension fund regulators don’t understand VC characteristics
- Education and Knowledge Transfer Challenges
- Family offices lack VC understanding
- Want peer learning spaces, not formal courses
- Need exposure to successful examples
- Accelerators teaching without proper expertise
- Mentors/staff never founded companies or raised capital
- Missing Silicon Valley knowledge transfer
- VC associations lack resources for education
- 20-30 member organizations can’t fund outreach to regulators/institutions
- Need public sector catalytic role initially
- Regional Talent and Investment Gaps
- Central America investment increased to $100-140M (not from local founders)
- Example: Biotech founder relocated to Próspera, Honduras, raised $40M seed
- Guatemala/Central America challenges:
- Capital available but lacks global-minded entrepreneurs
- Traditional entrepreneurs not building scalable startups
- Missing “mafia effects” from successful startup alumni
- Need 4-5 successful startups to activate ecosystem (per Endeavor research)
- Cultural bias toward foreign entrepreneurs
- Local investors more receptive to non-local founders
- “Malinchista” mentality favors external validation
- Proposed Solutions and Next Steps
- Public sector must lead initial education efforts
- Costa Rica opened Silicon Valley office for permanent knowledge transfer
- CAF, BID, BCIE have credibility and convocating power for multi-country initiatives
- Attract international entrepreneurs to region
- “Startup Chile” model without education component
- Focus on incentives: tax breaks, matching funds, quality of life
- More cost-effective than maintaining Silicon Valley offices

- PFA role in regional connectivity
- Address information flow gaps between neighboring countries
- Connect Brazilian/Mexican investors with Central American opportunities
