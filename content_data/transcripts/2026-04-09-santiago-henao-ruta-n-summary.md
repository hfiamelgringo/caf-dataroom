### Context

15 years working in Medellín's innovation ecosystem (founder, angel, corporate innovation, Georgia Tech program); ~2 years at Ruta N. **Medellín Venture Capital** is a city-level strategy launched late 2024 in partnership with the alcaldía and Empresas Públicas de Medellín. Total committed public capital: ~$50M, target $90–100M.

### Key Findings

- In 2024, Medellín startups raised only **$50–60M total** — described as "a very bad year"
- Critical insight: **97% of capital raised by Medellín startups in 2024 came from outside Medellín; only 3% local**. If exits happen, the capital recycles abroad — the Silicon Valley flywheel does not form
- Three-pronged program structure:
  1. **Formation**: 300+ angels, family-office members, and second/third-generation heirs trained since late 2024; partnerships with Babson College for VC certification
  2. **Community**: WhatsApp groups, monthly events, the "Smart Capital" event being relaunched in October. Spawned **Mei Capital**, a ~20-member angel syndicate, after participants found their preferred ticket sizes too small individually
  3. **Fund of Funds**: $90–100M target, modeled on Israel's Yozma, Mexico's Fondo de Fondos, Peru's COFIDE, and Chile's CORFO. Invests in regional micro-VCs that include Medellín startups in their portfolios
- Already deployed: $1.7M into **Bancoldex** (Colombia's national fund of funds), unlocking **$13M** for a microVC compartment; first investment $3.2M into Sima Fintech Plus, which is closing in on $10M to deploy across 30–35 fintech startups
- Built two open-data tools: **Inversómetro** (5-year VC tracking for Medellín / Colombia, identifying 15 exits in 5 years vs. the public perception of "none") and **Startia** (open-source ecosystem CRM)

### Regulatory Observations

- **Pension regulation is the unlock**: Mexico's Fondo de Fondos is the only LatAm fund-of-funds that successfully captured pension capital — and only after a regulatory change permitting pension investment in this asset class
- Family-office regulation around alternative-asset allocation could be a parallel lever

### Recommendations & Insights

- **Money follows money.** Foreign LPs will not invest in a country whose own residents won't. Local capital deployment is a precondition, not a consequence
- **Public sector should not invest directly** in startups. Catalyze via fund-of-funds and capacity building. Direct grants did not produce sustainable companies in Medellín's earlier era — the new bar is companies that "facturen, crezcan, y resuelvan un problema que valga la pena resolver"
- **Ecosystem narrative matters**: Medellín's perceived "no exits" reality, when researched, revealed 15 exits in 5 years. Surfacing this changes founder confidence
- **Open invitation for CAF to co-invest** in the Medellín VC fund of funds (CAF appeared as "potential" on the program's slide)
