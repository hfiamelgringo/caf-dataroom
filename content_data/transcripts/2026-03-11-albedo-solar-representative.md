## Context
Albedo Solar is a Guatemalan clean energy startup offering lease-to-own solar installations to SMEs across Guatemala, El Salvador, and Honduras, with a strong financial track record but significant capital access barriers. This interview surfaces first-hand evidence of structural financing gaps — illiquid local markets, risk-averse banking, slow impact fund processes, and VC models misaligned with 3x-return clean energy businesses — making it directly relevant to CAF's innovation and venture strategy work in the region.

## Notes
- Albedo Solar Company Overview
- Guatemalan solar startup, 7 years old, 200+ installations across Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras
- Lease-to-own model for SMEs
- 3-6 year contracts with 10% net return for clients
- Same monthly payment as current electric bill
- Equipment ownership transfers after contract completion
- 44% portfolio serves high social impact clients (women-led, youth-led, rural businesses)
- Market penetration only 2-2.5% in three countries
- Strong fundamentals: 3-4 year returns without subsidies vs 12-18 years in developed markets
- Started with friends/family ($20-50k tickets), progressed to family offices, angel investors
- Current: First priced equity round seeking $2M (raised $200k so far)
- Major bottlenecks identified:
- Illiquid markets - difficult jump from $50k to $200k tickets
- Local banks require personal/land guarantees despite strong portfolio
- Impact funds move slowly with long processes
- VC ecosystem focused on tech/software models seeking 30x returns vs their 3x model
- Payment rate: 0.3% default rate (extremely healthy)
- Legal/regulatory varies by country, requires navigation without clear playbooks
- No eligibility for government renewable incentives (designed for large projects)
- Regional holding company setup ongoing for ~1 year due to complex legal advice
- Banking system risk-averse, requires extensive track record
- Green mandate funding exists at banks but internal processes slow deployment
- Development Bank Recommendations
- Process improvements needed:
- Faster decision-making vs current 2-year timelines
- Smaller tickets ($200k) with quicker turnaround more valuable than $1M after long wait
- Bank guarantee programs to unlock existing green funds at local banks
- $$10-100M lines available but banks’ internal processes block deployment
- Guarantee would enable immediate capital access
- Subsidized consulting services for:
- Legal/tax structuring guidance
- Country expansion playbooks
- Capital raising navigation
- Regional holding company setup
- FLEA conferences highly valuable for networking and capital connections
- Palladium USAID accelerator provided crucial introductions for regional expansion
- Pomona accelerator (2023) most effective of 10+ accelerators attended
- Current priorities:
- Close $1.8M remaining in seed round (Q2 target)
- Unlock senior debt from Bidlab, OIQO post-equity
- Scale existing demand in 3 countries
- Expand to 5 additional countries: Panama, Dominican Republic, Colombia, Mexico
- Portfolio factoring/warehousing planned at $15-20M scale for recapitalization
