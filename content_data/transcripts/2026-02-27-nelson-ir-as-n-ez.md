## Context
Nelson Irías Núñez, 16-year ecosystem builder and founder of Costa Rica Tech Week and the Latin American Startup Alliance's Central American chapter, joined PFA team members Mark López (former IDB/USAID) and Rigoberto Romero (former Honduras Ministry of Finance) to assess the state of Central American startup ecosystems. The conversation surfaced systemic investment market failures, knowledge gaps among both founders and investors, and Nelson's phased model for building the region's first true innovation hub — directly relevant to CAF's venture and innovation strategy work in small Latin American countries.

- Central America Ecosystem Assessment
- Region is “in diapers” - very early stage compared to rest of Latin America
- Major knowledge gaps exist for both entrepreneurs and investors
- Entrepreneurs don’t understand investment fundamentals
- “VCs” are actually family offices making poor investment decisions
- Costa Rica leads slightly, followed by Guatemala and El Salvador
- El Salvador advancing due to government policy support
- Still 15-20 years behind Ecuador/Peru
- Investment Market Problems
- Destructive valuation practices damaging cap tables
- Example: Parque Tech (acquired by Marcus Dantus) offering $20K for 20% equity
- Creates unsalvageable cap tables for future rounds
- Inappropriate investment stages
- VCs investing in companies with only $1K monthly revenue
- Should be grant stage, not equity investment
- Non-standard practices
- Some funds requiring house guarantees for $120K investments
- Ego-driven competition preventing co-investment
- Goal: Launch 500 new startups annually in Central America by 2030
- Build first Central American innovation hub through Costa Rica Tech Week
- Current focus on founder development (not startups yet)
- Hackathons, startup weekends, bootcamps, competitions
- Three-stage development model:
- Grants for prototyping/MVP ($10-20K)
- Angel investor network for early revenue companies
- Institutional VCs for companies with $12K+ monthly revenue
- Ecosystem Infrastructure Needs
- Physical spaces for collaboration
- Regular events (bi-weekly) for networking and knowledge transfer
- Founder visa program for regional talent attraction
- Government innovation agencies (Costa Rica created theirs 3 years ago - Promotora de Innovación)
- Angel investor network development
- Connection to broader Latin American ecosystem
