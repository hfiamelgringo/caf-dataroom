## Executive Summary

The Rockefeller Foundation's Bogota regional office outlined a strategic pivot in their Central America programming: away from a three-way framing of climate, migration, and food systems, and toward a focused food systems mandate — particularly the intersection of regenerative agriculture and school meals procurement. They are planning a one-day stakeholder convening to be held in Guatemala, targeting participants from Honduras, El Salvador, and Guatemala, with private sector actors as the top priority, followed by multilaterals and large foundations, with governments ranked third due to political volatility in the region.

The Foundation signaled meaningful budget constraints, indicating the revised scope should come in at roughly half the original proposal, while offering to offset costs by assigning a new Bogota-based convening coordinator to join the planning team by March. The Foundation has no confirmed project in Central America yet but is actively exploring school meals supply-chain and regenerative agriculture opportunities, including an existing partnership in El Salvador focused on school meal quality and procurement. They are open to identifying one or two implementing partners for a potential grant if the convening surfaces strong candidates.

For CAF's purposes, this conversation reveals an active philanthropic actor seeking ecosystem intelligence, stakeholder mapping, and structured convening support in precisely the food systems and rural livelihoods space relevant to the dry corridor — a potential co-financing or co-convening partner worth tracking.

## Stakeholder Background

Andrea is a program officer with Rockefeller Foundation based in the Bogota regional office, focused on food systems programming in Latin America. Natalia is a recently joined colleague in the same office, designated to take on convening coordination responsibilities starting in March 2026. Andrea has been engaged in an iterative planning process with the PFA team since at least one prior call, and is positioned as the decision-maker for the Central America food systems portfolio at the regional level.

## Key Findings

### Food Systems Replaces the Climate-Migration-Food Trifecta as the Organizing Frame
Rockefeller Foundation is narrowing its Central America thematic focus from a three-part intersection of climate, migration, and food systems to food systems alone. The dry corridor remains the correct geographic lens, as food insecurity there is acknowledged to drive migration — but migration is no longer the headline. The shift reflects internal strategic realignment, not a change in geography.

### School Meals and Regenerative Agriculture Are the Two Priority Sub-Themes
Within food systems, the Foundation identified two investment-ready sub-themes: improving school meal quality and procurement (including sourcing from local producers), and supporting the transition to regenerative agriculture. An existing collaboration with a global foundation focused on El Salvador's school meals system is already in progress, with a local implementing partner still to be selected as of February 2026.

### Guatemala Is the Preferred Convening Location, with a Central America–Wide Participant List
The convening will be held in Guatemala, but must draw stakeholders from Honduras and El Salvador as well to avoid becoming a Guatemala-only event. Southern Mexico's inclusion was left open — the Foundation deferred to the organizing team on whether Chiapas-area actors could be included without diluting the Central America focus, citing the risk that broader Mexican representation tends to dominate regional conversations.

### Private Sector First, Governments Last in Participant Prioritization
When asked to rank audiences, Andrea placed private sector companies first, followed by multilaterals and large foundations, with governments ranked third. The explicit rationale was political volatility across the region and a desire to keep expectations calibrated — the Foundation anticipates it can fund one project or partner with one or two organizations, not many.

### Budget Is Being Cut Roughly in Half, with Line-Item Transparency Required
The Foundation indicated the revised budget should be approximately half the original proposal, but requested a detailed line-item breakdown before committing, noting that venue and logistics costs in Guatemala are fixed and need to be visible. The Foundation's new Bogota-based convening coordinator — starting by March at the latest — is intended to absorb some planning labor and reduce the external budget burden.

### One-Day Participatory Convening Replaces Pre-Meeting Interview Series
The Foundation prefers a single structured convening day with participatory session design — roundtables, breakouts, and group dynamics — over the originally proposed eight to ten semi-structured stakeholder interviews conducted in advance. The rationale is both logistical efficiency and expectation management: one-on-one meetings with the Foundation tend to generate disproportionate grant expectations that the team cannot fulfill.

### Post-Convening Output Should Be a Short Document, Not a Strategic Deliverable
The Foundation's expected output from the convening is a concise summary — described as "maybe two or three pages" — capturing key themes, areas of alignment, and any concrete agreements that emerged. They are not seeking project-specific recommendations or implementable deliverables, reflecting a listening and mapping posture rather than a deployment-ready funding strategy.

## Regulatory & Structural Observations

No regulatory or policy frameworks were discussed in substantive terms. The Foundation's reference to government stakeholders being ranked lowest due to "volatility in the region" implies awareness of political risk across the dry corridor countries, but no specific policies, laws, or institutional constraints were analyzed.

## Recommendations & Insights

The Foundation's primary ask of the organizing team is operational: revise the concept note to center food systems, cut scope to roughly half the budget, add line-item detail, and propose date options that avoid national holidays and school vacation periods in the three target countries — with a preferred timing around the start of the second semester (approximately July–August 2026). They want private sector participants to arrive well-briefed and ready to contribute, even if the formal pre-interview process is eliminated; a one-page briefing document distributed in advance was suggested as a lighter-touch alternative.

The Foundation is open to being introduced to organizations already active in the food systems space — including groups that have approached them proactively — and would welcome a stakeholder map as an input to the convening invite list. They noted a live pipeline in El Salvador around school meals and expressed openness to funding a Central America project in regenerative agriculture or school procurement if a strong candidate emerges from the convening.
