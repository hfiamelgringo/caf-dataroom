Fund of Funds Strategy for Latin America
* Deploy billions through multiple smaller entities rather than single large fund
* Focus on small checks, small funds approach
   * Avoids concentration in elite networks
   * Spreads wealth more broadly
   * Allows for more experimentation with lower downside risk
* Target seed and Series A investments rather than later stage only
   * Develops top of funnel ecosystem
   * Avoids betting only on “sure things” already at $10-30M revenue
Tax Policy Framework
* Provide tax credits on losses for both angel investors and funds
* Tie tax incentives to reinvestment requirements
   * Only provide benefits if capital stays in domestic market
   * Ensures exits benefit local ecosystem rather than just creating “tech bros in Miami”
* Multiple policy approaches available depending on government structure
Secondary Market Liquidity Solutions
* Government-sponsored entity provides liquidity for early investors in later rounds
   * 20-50% liquidity using market pricing from Series B/C rounds
   * Allows angels and seed funds to exit within 5-10 year timeframe
   * Addresses longer IPO timelines in current market
* Private secondary market approach (Dave’s current strategy):
   * $10M test portfolio across 8-10 companies doing $100M+ revenue
   * $1-2M positions buying from existing investors, employees, angels
   * Asset swap model: $1 equity → 20¢ cash + 80¢ diversified portfolio
   * Partial liquidity while maintaining upside exposure
Latin America Market Context
* SoftBank distorted market 2017-2019 with excessive capital
* Many strong companies started with too much funding, now seeking liquidity
* Very few players providing secondary market liquidity in LatAm venture
* Startup Chile punched above weight but was light on actual capital deployment
* Chilean fund of funds hitting 10-year repayment wall, creating secondary opportunities
Development Bank Recommendations
* Become better LPs by moving faster and writing smaller checks
* Reduce bureaucratic burden (current processes take 2-3 years for $10M funds)
* Heavy reporting requirements create friction for small fund deployment
* Diversification through many small bets reduces individual failure impact
* CAF’s new $20M impact fund shows movement toward fund of funds thinking
