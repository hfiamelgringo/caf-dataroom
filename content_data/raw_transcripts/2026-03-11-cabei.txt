VC Ecosystem Assessment for Central America
* Regional maturity levels vary significantly:
   * Tier 1: Costa Rica, Panama - more developed
   * Tier 2: Guatemala, El Salvador - similar to Chile/Mexico 10 years ago
   * Tier 3: Honduras, Nicaragua - need substantial development
* Key opportunities:
   * Young demographics across Central America
   * Strong intellectual capital potential
   * Financial inclusion gap represents major opportunity
* Current challenges:
   * Startups lack proper structuring for investors
   * Limited international investor attraction
   * Few specialized general partners in region
* CAF positioning aggressively in region:
   * $400-500M annually allocated to Honduras alone
   * Expanding to Guatemala, already in El Salvador
   * 70% growth target over 5 years
DFI Role & Fund Structure Recommendations
* MDBs should promote VC ecosystem beyond pure financial investment:
   * Link to development objectives (employment creation, financial inclusion)
   * Position VC as development tool for SDGs
   * Balance impact vs returns (3.5% FIDE returns too low)
* Regional fund-of-funds approach preferred:
   * Include multiple countries for scalability
   * Attract larger investors ($50M+ vs $10M)
   * Potential partners: CABEI, CDB, Fonplata, CAF
* Critical gaps to address:
   * Capacity building for local general partners
   * BID Invest developing GP training programs in Caribbean
   * Need specialized VC expertise, not just traditional fund management
* Startups must think regionally from inception:
   * Target Central America + Panama + Dominican Republic
   * Scale beyond local markets (example: Hogares.com expanding from El Salvador to Honduras)
