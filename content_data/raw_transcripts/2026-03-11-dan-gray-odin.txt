   2. Early-stage pipeline growth naturally creates middle-stage opportunities
   3. Government attempts to fund growth stage directly don’t work well

   1. Start with local angel investor ecosystems

Address the "missing middle" funding gap by focusing on early-stage pipeline growth, which will naturally create middle-stage opportunities. Avoid direct government attempts to fund the growth stage.

For capital-desert regions, start with local angel investor ecosystems, help successful angels build funds, and use asymmetric return structures like the British Business Bank model.

1. "Missing Middle" Funding Gap: The recommendation is to focus on building the early-stage pipeline rather than directly filling the middle-stage funding gap. By growing the base of early-stage investments, middle-stage opportunities will naturally emerge. Government attempts to directly fund the growth stage have not been effective.

2. Capital-Desert Regions: Begin by developing local angel investor ecosystems. Support successful angels in creating their own funds. Implement asymmetric return structures, similar to the British Business Bank model, to encourage investment. This involves offering favorable economics to other LPs who invest alongside the government or primary investor.

3. Network Effects: Concentrate resources geographically to create strong network effects. Avoid spreading resources too thinly across multiple regions, as this can dilute the impact and hinder the development of a robust ecosystem.

4. Tax Incentives: Tax incentives can be effective for angel investors, encouraging them to invest in early-stage companies. However, be cautious with tax incentives for funds, as they may lead to unintended consequences and do not always drive good investment behavior.
