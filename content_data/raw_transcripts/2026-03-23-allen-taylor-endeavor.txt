Endeavor's Regional Strategy for Central America Expansion
Endeavor Organization Overview
* Economic development organization focused on high-growth entrepreneurship
* Three core programs:
   * Access to markets (expansion support)
   * Access to talent (hiring for growth stages)
   * Access to capital (smart, connected venture capital)
* Operates in 50 countries with 600 full-time employees
   * Less than 5% have US passports
   * More Brazilians than any other nationality
* $800M fund making small investments ($few million) to signal quality to larger investors
Impact Philosophy & Approach
* Focus on small numbers of very big companies vs large numbers of smaller ones
* Target: GDP impact of 2-5% in small countries, 1-2% in large countries
* Three pillars of impact:
   1. Job creation (jobs of tomorrow/future)
   2. Wealth creation (local wealth that stays in market)
   3. Role model creation (inspiring next generation)
* Multiplier effect: 3-4 big companies create most impact over 20-25 years
* Uruguay example: Dlocal created multibillion dollar company from small country
Global Trends & Network Effects
* 20 years ago: 90% of companies were regional/national champions
* Today: Only 60% regional, 40% global from day one
* Network power more impactful than Silicon Valley exposure
   * Brazilian founders inspiring Nigerian founders
   * Peer-to-peer learning between similar-stage entrepreneurs
* “Pensar en grande” (think bigger) consistently top survey response from entrepreneurs
Central America Regional Strategy
* Exploring regional office model due to individual countries being too small
* Need $3M philanthropic funding for first 5 years
* Plus $10M investment capital for venture fund
* Business model: Carried interest from fund eventually funds operations
* Regional board structure: 1-2 business leaders from each country
* Key contacts already identified:
   * Costa Rica: Carikako Summit organizers
   * El Salvador: Creek family
   * Guatemala: Mata family
* Entrepreneurs already think regionally (several Central American founders in Mexico chapter)
Fund Structure & Performance
* Started 2012 after 15 years as mentorship-only organization
* Fund Zero: $10M → $86M DPI (8.6x return)
* Current: Fund Four, raising Fund Five ($300M vehicle)
* 400 equity investments in Endeavor companies
* 71 became billion-dollar businesses where backed at Series A/B
* Investment rules:
   * Never lead investor
   * Only co-invest with professional funds (not family offices)
   * Automatic investment in every Endeavor company meeting criteria
Next Steps
* Share Endeavor deck and regional expansion materials
* Explore partnership opportunities with CAF through PFA
* Continue conversations with Central American business leaders
* Consider similar regional models for Central Asia, Baltics, Ecuador/Peru/Colombia/Bolivia

The Central America Regional Strategy aims to establish a regional office model due to the small size of individual countries, making it more efficient to operate on a regional basis. The strategy requires $3 million in philanthropic funding for the first five years to get operations started, along with $10 million in investment capital for a venture fund. The business model is designed so that carried interest from the fund will eventually fund operations, reducing the need for continuous philanthropic support.
A regional board structure is proposed, with 1-2 business leaders from each country to ensure diverse representation and input. Key contacts have been identified to facilitate the strategy, including organizers of the Carikako Summit in Costa Rica, the Creek family in El Salvador, and the Mata family in Guatemala. Entrepreneurs in the region already have a regional mindset, with several Central American founders participating in the Mexico chapter, indicating a readiness for a regional approach.
